What I love about my hometown

KVW: Buenos Aires is a city full of life! No matter the day — weekdays, weekends, Holidays, the city is always alive. What I love the most about Buenos Aires is its Cultural Agenda, with a great offering that goes from classic and alternative theatre to live shows and musical events, as well as spectator sports. Of course, all of these followed by a hearty meal before or after!

BH: We have everything! If you want a casual steakhouse, we have it. If you want a fancy steak, we have those too. Sports, theater, food, even film festivals. If you get bored in Fort Worth, it’s 'cause you are staying home.

JP: Vibrant and varied food and music scenes are two of my favorite things about Seattle. Culturally, it has everything you want from a big city, with walkable neighborhoods and a more laid-back West Coast attitude, but with the mountains or coast right there for weekend adventures. Bonus points for seemingly endless summer days with no humidity.

And what I don't love

KVW: Some areas can be a little dicey, so it’s a good practice to learn what places to avoid as local or foreigner. Traffic can sometimes be a bit chaotic on business days, especially due to road blocks by groups of people protesting on the streets, which affects the normal flow of traffic.

BH: The traffic! Could be worse, I know. I also don’t love that Fort Worth gets a bad rap when compared to Dallas. It might be fancier in Dallas, but we have more fun in Fort Worth.

JP: Traffic: The geography that makes the city beautiful — steep hills, lakes, and the sound — can make driving a nightmare. Also, the persistent belief that it’s ok to wear Birks or “sports sandals” with wool socks post-college.

Underrated Tourist Attraction

KVW: Polo matches and Horse racing. Buenos Aires is known as the "Cathedral of Polo". There is no better place than this city to experience this wonderful and traditional sport in which you can admire our beautiful polo ponies and skillful polo players. Also the “Hipódromo Argentino de Palermo”, a beautiful horse racetrack with its Art Nouveau style buildings is definitely worth a visit!

BH: I really enjoy the Cultural District in Fort Worth. The Fort Worth Museum of Science and History is always fun for kiddos and it has the original IMAX/Omni movie theater and the Noble Planetarium. The Kimbell Art Museum is in the same area and there are some great restaurants nearby. The West 7th area and the Stockyards are close, too, if you don’t feel like a museum.

JP: Right near the Space Needle on the Seattle Center campus is La Marzocco Café, an excellent coffee bar that features beans from a different roaster from around the world on a monthly basis. The really cool thing: It’s also the headquarters for indie-hero KEXP radio station, which frequently hosts free live performances in the space. There’s a window into the DJ booth, roll-up garage doors for nice summer and fall days, comfy seating, and an on-site record store from Light in the Attic Records. It’s the epitome of everything that originally appealed to me about Seattle, hidden just out of view of one of its busiest tourist traps.

Favorite Local Restaurant

KVW: I love Spanish tapas, so my favorite local restaurant is El Burladero: tapas, jabugo ham, gazpacho, olives, paella, seafood, tortilla, oxtail, and of course cider, beer and excellent Argentine or Spanish wine. Note that, as a good “argentina” I do have a favorite meat restaurant, the very famous La Brigada.

BH: Joe T Garcia’s Mexican restaurant is my happy place. It’s in the Stockyards and is still run by the same family that started it. The actual restaurant was originally the family house and the fountain in the middle of the patio used to be the kids pool while the adults served the food. They don’t even have a menu. The servers just tell you they have enchiladas or fajitas as entrees. But the margaritas are better than the food, I think. To me, it feels like a hideaway in the Stockyards. I can sit for hours with a margarita and nachos and be completely happy!

I’m always happy at Café Presse or Oddfellows for lunch, or Bar Melusine for oysters. But if you’re visiting from out of town: Westward for great PNW seafood, Lake Union views looking back at the city, and a dining room loosely themed on The Life Aquatic — arrive early for a drink in the Adirondack chairs by the bonfire.

Favorite Weekend Activity

KVW: A morning bicycle ride in the Palermo parks followed by lunch with family or friends. Lunch may be at a local favorite restaurant or an “asado” (barbecue) at someone’s home which continues with a late afternoon round of “mate” (sort of tea infusion) and “facturas” (local sweet pastries).

BH: When the weather is nice, I love sitting on a patio drinking margaritas and people watching. I try to see plays and shows at Bass Hall when I can. If it is super-hot, I will be seeing a movie or hanging out in someone’s pool. If it’s really cold, I won’t even leave my house.

JP: Something outside: skiing or snowboarding December through May, cycling the rest of the time. I’m currently learning to not flip a sculling shell.

How to spot a native

KVW: There are a few ways to spot a native: we are loud and like to show off, the word “che” will reveal a native (It´s like saying “hey you,” like Hey you, what do you think if this? Hey you, would you like to go and have dinner?), we also tend to use the word “viste” (did you notice/see) at the end of many phrases. Soccer (we call it football) will come up in almost every male conversation and women are recognized for good taste in fashion.

BH: Usually, you can tell by how they talk if they are native. If someone uses “Y'all” or “fixin'” correctly, you know they are a native. If you see someone in a cowboy hat, boots and jeans, they are usually a native — transplants can’t pull off all three. It’s not a visual thing.

JP: They’re the ones walking in the rain with beanies for umbrellas November through April.
