KIXEYE Finds More Time to Make Kickass Games

While most gaming companies around the Bay Area struggle to stay afloat, online gaming company KIXEYE is adding around 30 new people each month. KIXEYE makes competitive online combat strategy games for the mid-to-hardcore gaming crowd, releasing feature updates on a weekly basis to its passionate community of over five million. KIXEYE's flagship titles include Backyard Monsters, Battle Pirates, and War Commander.

“Managing a live product means balancing lots of priorities,” said Sarah Levantine, senior product manager for KIXEYE. “You’ve got live bugs you have to address as soon as possible, long-term roadmap items, and all the while, you have to keep your players interested all the time.”

The sheer number of tickets and growth rate of the company prompted Kevin Bernstein, director of IT, to further invest in and consolidate on Atlassian's products. Previously, KIXEYE was using a mashup of different tools, but thanks to Bernstein's prior experience with Atlassian products, he knew JIRA could be used for much more than tracking software bugs and features.

“When I joined there were 100 people, and within a few months there were 200. We needed a new solution that would scale with the company to take on IT ticketing, bug tracking, and other non-technical requests,” he said.

Nowadays, a whopping 90 percent of KIXEYE uses JIRA on a daily basis. Most departments, software development-related or not, use it to bring transparency and metrics to all their project tracking.

Kenn White, the project manager for a “top-secret” new game at KIXEYE, says JIRA has helped his team of developers become 30 percent more accurate with their time estimates.
